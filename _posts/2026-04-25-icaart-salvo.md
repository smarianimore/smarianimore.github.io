Dear fellow researchers, the paper that [Salvo Finistrella](https://www.linkedin.com/in/salvo-finistrella-03083540/), 
brilliant PhD student collaborating with me and [Franco Zambonelli](http://www.agentgroup.unimore.it/Zambonelli/), 
published at [ICAART 2026](https://icaart.scitevents.org/?y=2026) is now available :) 

Check it out [here](https://www.scitepress.org/Link.aspx?doi=10.5220/0014300900004052).

![image-center]({{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/2026-icaart-salvo-glance.png){: .align-center}

In that paper, Salvo put to test his own brand new simulator [MininetGym](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S235271102500278X), 
by comparing tabular and deep RL algorithms on two cybersecurity tasks. 

![image-center]({{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/2026-icaart-salvo-eval-detection.png){: .align-center}

In particular, he also analysed the kind of errors that the learnt policies made, 
so as to provide a practically usable assessment of RL capabilities within the cybersecurity domain.

![image-center]({{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/2026-icaart-salvo-errors.png){: .align-center}

Finally, Salvo summarised the main takeaway lessons learned: 

  - ✅ Traffic classification and attack detection can be dealt with by RL
  - 📉 Better training performance does not necessarily correlate to better generalisation
  - ⚖️ Reward design should carefully balance false alarms with missed threats

Feel free to [contact me](mailto:stefano.mariani@unimore.it) for a pre-print, any inquiry, or even better **to join us** in this research :)

Peace.
